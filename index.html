Mar Baselios College of Engineering and Technology

DUCIN ALTEM

Mar Baselios College of Engineering and Technology (MBCET) was established in the year 2002 by the Malankara Catholic Church and is administered by the Malankara Catholic Educational Society of the Major Arch Diocese of Thiruvananthapuram. Situated amidst 17 educational institutions in the Mar Ivanios Vidyanagar, just 5 km from the heart of the city, MBCET stands tall as a pride to one and all. Mar Baselios College of Engineering & Technology strives for excellence in higher education, research and support services, by employing an integral approach to education, with the purpose of forming technically competent engineers, responsible citizens and epitomes of fundamental human values like commitment, compassion and optimism, who ought to be actively involved in creative nation building and enhancement of human existence. The college has five engineering departments, Civil Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering, Electrical & Electronics Engineering, Electronics & Communication Engineering and Mechanical Engineering, catering to the requirements of the industry and society at large.

Electronics and Communications Department

Mar Baselios College of Engineering and Technology

The Department of Electronics & Communication Engineering focuses on imparting a solid foundation, both theoretical and hands on work experience, across the vast spectrum of technologies in Electronics as well as Communication Engineering. The department has a team of well qualified and competent faculty who are active in research and research guidance with a number of publications to their credit. To encourage research and development, the Mar Baselios College of Engineering & Technology annually organizes International level Conferences and Seminars.After the overwhelming success of Broadband ’08 and CoMNeT2010, organized by the department, we are organizing an International Conference on Communication Systems and Networks (CoMNeT-2016) during 21st to 23rd of July 2016 in association with University of Dayton, Ohio, USA. This three-day conference will promise to be a much larger one and this will offer an opportunity both to interact with and listen to leaders from the industry and academia from India and abroad.

IEEE Conference on Comminication Networks

COMNET 2016

The IEEE International Conference on Communication Systems and Networks2016 (COMNET-2016) is organized by Department of Electronics & Communication Engineering, Mar Baselios College of Engineering & Technology, Thiruvananthapuram- 695015, India, from July 21 to 23, 2016. COMNET-2016 serves as a forum for the researchers, industry professionals and academicians interested in the latest development and design of Communication Systems and Networks.

One of the major goals of IEEE COMNET 2016 is to promote an intense dialogue between academia and industry to bridge the gap between academic research, industry initiatives, and governmental policies. This is fostered through panel discussions, keynotes and invited talks where academia is exposed to state-of-the-art research activities. The industry in turn benefits by exposure to leading-edge research in communication systems and networks. It also provides the opportunity to communicate with academic researchers regarding practical problems that require further research. .

University Of Dayton

The University of Dayton (UD) is a top-tier Catholic research university with academic offerings from the undergraduate to the doctoral levels. Founded in 1850 by the Society of Mary, it is one of three Marianist universities in the nation and the largest private university in Ohio. The university's campus is located in the city's southern portion and spans 388 acres on both sides of the Great Miami River. The University of Dayton has about 8,000 undergraduate and 3,500 post-graduate students from a variety of religious, ethnic and geographic backgrounds, drawn from across the United States and more than 40 countries. It offers more than 80 academic programs in arts and sciences, business administration, education and health sciences, engineering, law and, in 1988, was first in the country to offer an undergraduate degree program in human rights. University of Dayton ranks #1 among all Catholic Universities in the USA in sponsored engineering research, and #2 in Materials Research in the Country. The University of Dayton Research Institute (UDRI) research staff and UD faculty jointly manage over $90 Million each year in research funding.

Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering

University of Dayton

The Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at the University of Dayton offers world-class programs leading to the degrees of Bachelor of Electrical Engineering and Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering. Both degrees are accredited by the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET). Electrical & Computer Engineering along with Electro-Optics (EO) graduate program offers three masters level (MS in Electrical Engineering, MS in Computer Engineering and MS in Electro Optics), and two doctoral level (PhD in ECE and PhD in EO) degree programs and has an involvement of 18 full-time faculty members, and approximately 330 undergraduate and 270 graduate students, including 67 PhD students. Faculty expertise in Electrical and Computer Engineering includes topics such as biomedical/bioengineering, computer hardware and software, computer vision, digital system design and integrated circuit design, electro-optics, robotics and controls, sensor technologies, signal and image processing, telecommunications and wireless systems, and wide area surveillance.

The Dayton area is a high-tech research and development community, and our faculty work with nearby Wright-Patterson Air Force Base (WPAFB), Air Force Research Laboratories (AFRL), and numerous aerospace, automobile, sensor systems, and information technology companies. The UD campus is now home to the new $53 million General Electric Electrical Power Integrated Systems Center (EPISCenter), and the new Emerson's Innovation Center known as the "Helix", with close ties to our department. In addition to state of the art teaching labs, we have a number of exciting specialty labs such as the new Mumma Radar Lab, the Computer Vision Lab, Integrated Microsystems Lab, High Performance Computing Lab, Embedded Systems Lab, and the Motoman Robotics lab, and numerous faculty research labs with active undergraduate and graduate research projects. The ECE department faculty include world-renowned teachers and researchers who have authored textbooks, published extensively in prestigious journals and conferences, and are Fellows in their respective professional organizations. The world-class academic curriculum in ECE at UD is flexible and updated regularly to meet the changing needs of government, industry, and academia.

Rules

The primary focus of the conference is on new and original research results in the areas of design, implementation, and applications of communications and networks. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Communication Systems
  • Signal Processing
  • Computer Vision and Artificial Intelligence

All the accepted papers will be included in the conference proceedings and will be submitted for inclusion in the IEEE Xplore.
The format instructions in the template must be followed, it is notably important to use the right paper format with right margins and not to use page numbering (page footer must be empty).
Authors are expected to submit manuscripts with original research results that have not been previously published or that are not currently under review by another conference or journal.
Submissions will be judged based on originality, significance, clarity, relevance, and correctness. All submitted papers will be subjected to peer reviews by Technical Program Committee members and other experts in the field.

Authors are requested to submit papers of four to six A4 pages (including tables, figures and references) in standard IEEE double-column format (it is absolutely necessary to respect the Styleguide for Papers). A blind peer-review process will be used to evaluate all submitted papers.Manuscripts must be submitted electronically in PDF format through Login

Prospective authors are invited to submit original technical papers for publication in the IEEE COMNET 2016. Authors are requested to submit their file in the format specified in the IEEE Paper Template.
Important IEEE Policy Announcement: The IEEE reserves the right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference (including its removal from IEEE Xplore) if the paper is not presented at the conference.
Papers are reviewed on the basis that they do not contain plagiarized material and have not been submitted to any other conference at the same time (double submission). These matters are taken very seriously and the IEEE will take action against any author who engages in either practice. Follow these links to learn more:
